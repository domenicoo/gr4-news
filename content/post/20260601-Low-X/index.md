> “Diffraction and Low-x 2026” is a joint effort between the Diffraction and Low-x workshop series.
> The purpose of this workshop is to stimulate discussions between experimentalists and theorists
> in diffractive hadronic physics, QCD dynamics at low-x, parton saturation and exciting problems
> in QCD at HERA, Tevatron, LHC, RHIC and the future EIC.
> 
> Diffraction is a biennial workshop dedicated to theoretical and experimental progress in diffractive
> processes at high energies.
> Low-x is an annual workshop whose spirit is to favour fruitful and informal discussions between
> experimentalists and theorists in low-x, diffractive hadronic physics and exciting problems in QCD
> at HERA, Tevatron and LHC.
> 
> Diffraction and Low-x covers a broad list of topics, organized in six sessions:
> 
> A) Diffraction in e-p and e-ion collisions: Inclusive DIS: total cross sections, structure functions,
> heavy flavors; Inclusive diffraction and dijets in DIS; Hard diffractive photoproduction;
> Exclusive final states in diffractive DIS (vector mesons, DVCS, etc.); Analyticity/duality models
> of inclusive/diffractive reactions; Pomeron trajectory; Diffraction at the EIC; Forward detectors
> and Roman pots.
> 
> B) Diffraction in p-p and A-A collisions: Soft diffraction at the hadron colliders; Total and
> inelastic cross section measurements; Odderon physics; Central exclusive production of low-mass
> particles; Glueball production; Forward physics at the LHC; Forward detectors; Phenomenology
> of gap survival probability; Monte Carlo for soft processes; Heavy ion collisions at RHIC and EIC;
> Phenomenology of diffraction off nuclei; Hard diffraction at the hadron colliders; Monte Carlo for
> hard processes.
> 
> C) Ultraperipheral collisions and gamma-gamma physics: Gamma-proton and gammagamma
> collisions at hadron colliders; Beyond standard model and photon exchange processes;
> Ultraperipheral collisions.
> 
> D) Spin Physics: New results on spin physics; Spin and polarization physics; Prospects in spin
> physics; Generalized parton distributions; Transverse momentum dependent PDFs.
> 
> E) Results on QCD and Hadronic Final States: pQCD predictions; Jet cross sections and
> algorithms; Jet substructures; Multiplicities; Vector meson, pion, etc. productions; Formal developments
> in QCD (resummation, factorization, evolution); New results in the BFKL physics;
> New results in the color dipole/kt-factorization approach; Progress in AdS/QCD, integrability
> and related topics.
> 
> F) Low x, PDFs, and saturation: PDFs at low and high x; New results within the Color Glass
> Condensate model; Saturation and evolution; Saturation in heavy ion.
> Although the accent is put on the study of diffractive processes, i.e. processes governed by the exchange
> of vacuum quantum number between the colliding particles (which can be leptons, photons,
> hadrons and nuclei), and physics at low values of the Bjorken x variable, as a matter of fact the
> Workshop covers many aspects of the Strong Interactions, ranging from soft collisions described by
> Regge models to hard ones described by perturbative QCD, the latter including the high-density
> regime, particularly relevant in nuclear collisions, where novel phenomena could show up. Special
> attention will be devoted to (generalized) parton distribution functions, including polarized ones.
