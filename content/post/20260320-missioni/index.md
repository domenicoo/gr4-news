> Dear Colleagues,
> 
> We are writing to inform you of several updates introduced by the Italian Tax Agency (Agenzia delle Entrate) in Circular No. 15/E dated December 22, 2025, concerning the treatment of mission expenses and reimbursements.
> 
> 1) For INFN employees only:
> The Tax Agency has introduced a mandatory requirement for Public Administrations to verify the "debt position" (tax compliance) of a beneficiary before issuing payments exceeding €2,500.00 related to the indemnity/remuneration component. This means that for missions under a flat-rate meal allowance or per diem regime—where the meal or per diem amount exceeds a net total of €2,500—the office must perform the aforementioned compliance checks.
> 
> 2) For all personnel (Employees, Associates, Research Fellows, Scholarship Holders, Guests, etc.):
> 
>     Parking Receipts: Parking expense vouchers must now explicitly and uniquely state the vehicle license plate number and the duration of the stay. If these details are missing, the reimbursement will be subject to taxation.
> 
>     Tourist Tax (Italy): Tourist taxes for missions within Italy are now subject to traceability regulations. Consequently, they must be paid via electronic means (credit card, bank transfer, etc.). Any tourist tax paid in cash will be treated as taxable income.
>
